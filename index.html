About Me

Learnmore Kumbirai Magorimbo is an African entrepreneur, founder, writer, and Pan-African youth activist committed to building practical African solutions through business, innovation, and leadership.

He is the owner and director behind multiple ventures including MyWorka, TripBot, Wash N Go Cleaning Services, Maq Ruwan, and Gate Garage Welding. His work reflects a strong passion for enterprise development, job creation, industrial growth, service excellence, logistics innovation, and African economic transformation.

Learnmore’s entrepreneurial vision is rooted in empowering communities, supporting workers, developing African-owned brands, and creating systems that solve real everyday problems in transport, services, welding, manufacturing, and digital marketplaces.

Entrepreneurial Ventures

MyWorka – A platform concept designed to connect workers, service providers, and clients while helping empower the informal and skilled workforce.

TripBot – A transport and logistics concept focused on load coordination, trips, and movement of goods across markets.

Wash N Go Cleaning Services – A professional cleaning company serving homes and businesses with quality cleaning solutions.

Maq Ruwan – An ambitious automotive and industrial vision focused on African manufacturing, engineering, and vehicle development.

Gate Garage Welding – A welding and fabrication business providing gates, structural work, security installations, and metal solutions.

Political Journey and Activism

From a very young age, Learnmore became involved in political and civic activism, driven by a commitment to justice, democracy, constitutionalism, and accountable leadership in Africa.

His journey in activism included participation in youth and democratic movements, political involvement, and Pan-African youth engagement. Along the way, he endured persecution and torture, experiences that deepened rather than weakened his dedication to freedom, dignity, and the rights of African people.

He remains committed to the vision of a united, empowered, and self-determined Africa, and sees himself as a citizen of Africa’s shared future.
